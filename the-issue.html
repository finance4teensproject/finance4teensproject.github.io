The Issue

Many teens are unprepared for real-life financial challenges. Understanding money early can prevent future debt, stress, and missed opportunities.

Financial literacy is not just about math—it's about making smart decisions with your money, planning for the future, and knowing your options. Many teens graduate high school without essential money skills, which can lead to debt, low savings, and poor credit habits.

By understanding these issues now, teens can gain confidence and independence, avoid common financial mistakes, and build a strong foundation for adult life.

Why This Matters

Lack of financial education can lead to:

  • Accumulating unnecessary debt
  • Low credit scores, affecting loans and housing
  • Poor budgeting habits that hinder saving
  • Stress and anxiety around money decisions

Providing teens with clear, practical resources helps them take control of their finances and avoid these common pitfalls.
