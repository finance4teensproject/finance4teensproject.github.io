Credit

Understanding credit is essential for building a strong financial foundation. Credit affects your ability to buy a car, get a loan, or even rent an apartment.

Core Lessons

What is Credit?

Credit is borrowing money now and paying it back later. Credit cards, loans, and lines of credit are common examples.

Credit Score Basics

Your credit score is a number that represents how responsible you are with money. Paying bills on time, keeping balances low, and managing accounts wisely improves your score.

Using Credit Wisely

Always pay at least the minimum balance on time, avoid maxing out cards, and only borrow what you can repay.

Examples & Scenarios

  • Alex opens a credit card and pays off the full balance each month to build credit without paying interest.
  • Maria misses a payment and her score drops, making future loans more expensive.

Activities & Practice

1. Check your spending habits: If you had a credit card, how would you pay it off each month?

2. Scenario: You want to borrow $500. You can pay $50/month. How long will it take if the interest rate is 12%?

3. Quiz yourself on what actions help or hurt credit scores.
