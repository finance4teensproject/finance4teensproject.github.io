Budgeting & Saving

Learning to manage money is a skill you’ll use for life. Budgeting and saving helps you reach your goals and avoid unnecessary stress.

Core Lessons

Saving Basics

Short-term savings are for things you want soon, medium-term savings are for months ahead, and long-term savings are for major goals like college or a car.

Creating a Budget

Track your income and expenses. Make categories like “Essentials,” “Fun,” and “Savings.” Example: You earn $50/week, spend $20 on snacks, $10 on transport, $10 saved, $10 fun money.

Tracking Spending

Use a spending journal, apps, or envelopes to stay on top of where your money goes. Review weekly and adjust your plan if needed.

Examples & Scenarios

  • Emma gets $40/week allowance. She saves $15/week for a new bike.
  • Jamal works part-time and tracks expenses using a spreadsheet to save for college books.
  • Li spends $5/day on coffee but decides to cut back to save $20/month for a concert.
