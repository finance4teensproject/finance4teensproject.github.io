College Costs & Loans

College can be expensive, but planning ahead helps you save and borrow wisely. Learn about tuition, loans, and ways to reduce costs.

Core Lessons

Understanding Tuition & Fees

Tuition varies by school. Include housing, meals, and supplies when budgeting for college.

Student Loans

Loans help pay for college but must be repaid with interest. Federal loans usually have lower interest than private loans.

Ways to Save

Scholarships, grants, part-time jobs, and early savings reduce the amount you need to borrow.

Examples & Scenarios

  • Lily gets a $2,000 scholarship, reducing her tuition from $12,000 to $10,000.
  • Marcus works part-time during the semester and saves $3,000 over the year to pay for books and meals.

Activities & Practice

1. Calculate your college budget: Tuition + Housing + Supplies + Miscellaneous.

2. Explore 3 scholarship options online and note eligibility requirements.

3. Scenario: You need $5,000 for the semester. Federal loan offers 5% interest, private loan 10%. Which is cheaper?
